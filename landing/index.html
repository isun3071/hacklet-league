# the format

Hackathon, but minutes instead of hours. With a crowd watching. You get 24 minutes and one mid-tier AI to build a working web app and write it up. No web search, no second AI, no pasting from Stack Overflow. What's left is how well you drive the model, and whether you catch it when it's wrong.

At zero, the network drops and your code freezes. Whatever the AI was mid-sentence on gets rolled back. A commit grabs exactly what was there at the buzzer, and that's your submission. Then it gets attacked.

# anatomy of a round ~120 min · 12 players

T-5:00opening. intros, framing, last-minute nerves.
T+0:00build starts. you, the AI, and a 24-minute clock.
T+24:00freeze. the network cuts and the central fuzzer runs every test, public and hidden.
T+36:00pitch. 60 seconds to sell it, 60 more answering to the judges.
T+66:00judges deliberate while the crowd votes for people's hacklet.
T+81:00awards. categories first, best overall last.
