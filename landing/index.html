The format

Hackathon, but minutes instead of hours — with a cheering audience. Players get 24 minutes and a single mid-tier AI model to build, document, and defend a web application. No web search. No second AI tool. No copy-paste from outside. Just judgment, prompting fluency, and nerve.

When the clock hits zero, the network cuts and the code freezes — partial AI responses roll back, and an automatic commit captures exactly what existed at the buzzer. Then the real test begins.

How a round works

  1. 01

    Build · 24 min

    Direct the AI. Write code in your own IDE. Probe your own defenses with live fuzz tests, and spend a finite token budget wisely. The room watches your score climb in real time.

  2. 02

    Fuzz · the gauntlet

    At freeze, your app faces the full catalog: SQL injection, XSS, auth bypass, malformed input, encoding tricks, the works. Defended earns points. Broken loses them. Hidden tests stay hidden until the reveal.

  3. 03

    Defend · pitch & cross-ex

    60 seconds to pitch what you built. 60 seconds under judge questioning. Then categorical awards and the composite champion — Most Resilient, Best Pitch, Best Overall, and the audience's People's Hacklet.

The models do the typing. The judgment is yours.

Anyone can vibe-code a demo. HackLet measures whether it holds up when something attacks it — the AI-complementary skills that don’t get easier as the models get better: engineering judgment, verification reflex, resource calibration, defense under questioning.
